About HackTrin

HackTrin VI is the sixth iteration of Trinity School’s annual hackathon and is on Sunday, March 3rd. Middle and high schoolers, of any experience level, can participate. You can form teams of 1-4 students, even across different schools, and work to make projects relating to coding. We'll have workshops and mentors to help you with your projects and learn! You’ll present your projects to our panels of judges to win prizes in multiple categories (including a beginner category)!

FAQ


What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where teams get together and build a project relating to coding, using software or hardware components, in a short period of time.

What types of projects are allowed at HackTrin VI?

Anything you want, as long as it involves some coding component and relates to the theme. Popular projects in the past have included websites, apps, games, gagdets, or whatever else you come up with!

Are teacher chaperones required?

Yes, for insurance purposes, Trinity requires one teacher chaperone per school. Even if you have multiple teams from one school, you only need one chaperone. Please email us if that's an issue for you!

Is there a theme at HackTrin VI?

We're announcing the theme the day of the hackathon.

Who can come to HackTrin VI?

HackTrin VI is open to any current high school or middle school students. Middle school students will compete in a special division, HackTrin Junior.

What is different about HackTrin Junior?

HackTrin Junior is only open to current middle school students. HackTrin Junior will start at 2:00, but middle and high school students will come together at the end of the day for presentations, awards, and prizes.


Will there be food at HackTrin VI?

Yes, we will provide breakfast and lunch, as well as snacks throughout the day. (We will accomodate any dietary restrictions.)
